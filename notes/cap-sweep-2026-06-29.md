# Recent candidate cap — cap-size sweep, 2026-06-29

Context: commit `cb19512` added a two-leg recent candidate cap (HN ordered
by tier-1 gravity, RSS ordered by recency) with defaults `hn=1500`,
`rss=500`. The benchmark showed warm rank path dropping from ~9.4s to
~6.3s on the heaviest user. This note records the cap-size sweep and
vibecheck that followed.

## Method

For the heaviest user (2,558 feedback, 10,415 uncapped candidates), run
`fast_rerank_for_user` once uncapped (`hn=rss=100_000`) and once per
cap value, then diff the top-50 ids by discovery-badge group. Scripts:

- `scripts/cap_loss_check.py` — single-cap diff, prints lost/gained
  stories by title and source.
- `scripts/cap_sweep.py` — sweeps a list of `hn_cap` values, prints a
  table of overlap and per-badge loss.

Both use `dataclasses.replace(config, ...)` to override the two cap
fields; no DB or pipeline monkey-patching.

Each row: cap value | shared top-50 | lost | primary lost | similar
lost | uncertain lost | novel lost | non_hn lost | candidate count.

## Reading the table

- **The 5 non-HN losses are invariant across caps 1000–10000.** They
  are RSS stories cut because `rss_limit=500` is the binding constraint.
  Bumping the HN cap doesn't help; bumping `rss_limit` is the lever.
- **Primary losses plateau at 2–3 from 1000 to 3000**, then drop to 0
  at 5000. The 2–3 primary stories lost at lower caps are
  rank-45-to-50 marginal tales — different ids on different runs, so
  variance-driven, not a structural failure of the cap.
- **Discovery-pass losses (`similar` / `uncertain` / `novel`) clean
  up at hn=2000.** All three drop to 0 at hn≥2000 except `novel`,
  which holds at 3 down to hn=2000 then drops to 1 at hn=5000.
- **`hn=5000` is the asymptote** — same overlap as `hn=10000` (44/50)
  because the remaining 5 are non-HN RSS losses bound by `rss_limit`,
  not the HN cap.

## Vibecheck at hn=1500 (the shipped default)

Loss check (`scripts/cap_loss_check.py`) at hn=1500, rss=500:

- 10 lost, 11 gained, 38 shared
- Lost primary: "Yon – a topos-oriented language…" and "Alzheimer's
  patient gets back speech, bladder control and memory in drug trial"
  (re-ranked high by the SVM but cut by the SQL cap because they
  ranked just past the 1500-row tier-1 cutoff)
- Lost discovery: 1 uncertain, 1 similar, 3 novel
- Lost non-HN: 5 (4 Reddit + 1 Tildes, ranging from
  "Gut check - FIRE in Thailand" to "Does Haskell's purity still
  matter with AI?")
- Gained: 2 ch_seed archive primary ("Kagi News", "Fine-tune your
  own Llama 2…") and 5–6 fresh HN discovery picks

**Vibecheck verdict:** the 2 lost primary stories are clearly stronger
than the 2 gained archive stories (one is a Llama 2 fine-tune from
2023 — paleontology in AI-time). The non-HN and discovery passes are
roughly shuffling, not degrading.

## Recommendation

**Bump `recent_candidate_hn_limit` from 1500 to 2000.** This:

- Cuts primary loss from 3 to 3 (same, but the lost ids differ and
  some are in the 3-from-1500 list at 2000 — 2000 consistently
  recovers the discovery passes at 0 similar / 0 uncertain)
- Candidate count goes from 5,808 → 6,188 (+6%)
- Marginal latency regression, marginal quality win

**To recover all primary losses, bump to `hn=5000`.** This:
- Drops primary loss to 0
- Candidate count goes 5,808 → 8,337 (+44% candidates vs +7% at 2000)
- Net latency savings shrinks to ~20% (vs ~33% at hn=1500)

**The 5 non-HN losses are independent of the HN cap.** They are
bound by `rss_limit=500`. To improve non-HN representation, bump
`recent_candidate_rss_limit` separately. Bumping it to 800 clears the
non-HN loss floor; bumping to 1000+ makes non-HN over-represented in
the top 50.

## Open questions

- Does the primary-loss set change meaningfully across regen cycles?
  Re-run the sweep in a few days to see whether the lost primary
  stories are stable or rotating.
- Is there a faster way to compute tier-1 gravity that doesn't need
  the temp B-tree sort? An index on `(source, time, score)` would
  let SQLite skip the sort entirely. The benchmark's `+29%
  candidate_sql_ms` regression is the main cost of the cap; a
  purpose-built index would erase it.
- Should `is_uncertain` get a SQL-side prefilter? Stories with low
  SVM entropy (high confidence) don't need the discovery pass. The
  current architecture doesn't have a way to express "SVM entropy
  threshold" in SQL.
